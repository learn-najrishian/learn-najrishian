Chapter 1

Alphabet

Vowels

The vowels in Najrishian are written in Najrishian. But for now, I'm going to tell you the sounds.
A. This sound is similar to the "a" in the word "father".

Try it out now.
The other vowels consist of U, O, and UI. Along with LALU and I.
In order, these sound like "oo", the "o" in "oh", "oo-ee", and I sounds like "ee".
The vowel LALU is hard to replicate. Try flicking your tongue upwards and downwards on the roof
of your mouth while saying "lalu".

Try it. You'll get the hang of it soon enough!

Consonants

The consonants are B, D, F, G, H, KH, K, L, M, N, P, R, S, T, Z.
These sounds sound like their English counterpart except for G and KH.
KH sounds like you're trying to hock out some mucus. It is the same as a French R and German R.
G sounds like a mixture of G, D, and K. A soft K with a hint of G and D. Good luck!

Puncuation and Phonetic Markings

Puncuation is the same as English except for space. When you're done with a word, you put a | in between it.
Like so. maKHRa | la | simenTalaHa. Notice how the emphasis is on the capitalized letters.
In Najrishian, this accent is displayed with a Q shape above or below the letter, depending on whether it's
a vowel or not. Vowels have it below, consonants above. Super easy right!
